In the event of climate overshoot, where global warming peaks above the Paris Agreement 1.5°C goal, limits to adaptation are more likely to be exceeded. The climate risk dashboard provides information on higher warming scenarios and [limits to adaptation](/adaptation#illustrative-adaptation-pathways) with overshoot impact projections to help guide adaptation planning and policy decisions. 

### How to use dashboard data in adaptation planning 

Different frameworks already exist to illustrate steps in adaptation planning, including the [Adaptation Support Tool](https://climate-adapt.eea.europa.eu/en/knowledge/tools/adaptation-support-tool/index_html) and [Adaptation Policy Framework](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/305389848_Adaptation_policy_frameworks_for_climate_change_developing_strategies_policies_and_measures). While such steps differ slightly between sources, most include the three simple elements listed below: 

1. **Assess the current context and risks** for a specified area or group. This can include the current climate, as well as socioeconomic and development trends. Common sources for data could include governmental statistics.  [See examples](/adaptation/nassau#context)
2. **Assess future risks**, including what current risks could be aggravated, or new risks introduced, in future scenarios. The climate risk dashboard’s [Future impacts mode](https://climate-risk-dashboard.climateanalytics.org/impacts/explore), [Avoid future impacts mode](https://climate-risk-dashboard.climateanalytics.org/impacts/avoid), and [Overshoot policy self-assessment tool](/adaptation#overshoot-proofing-self-assessment-tool) can inform this step. [See examples](/adaptation/nassau#future-heat-stress-in-nassau)
3. **Identify adaptation options** according to assessed risks. Dashboard data can inform the design of locally led studies to prioritise between options. Our team also can be [commissioned](/adaptation#provide-publications-on-adaptation) to carry out urban level studies. [See examples](/adaptation/nassau#adapting-to-heat-with-nature-based-solutions-in-nassau)

We include [case study examples](/adaptation/nassau) to illustrate these three basic steps and outline how climate risk dashboard data informed such studies.
