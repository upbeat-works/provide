Population exposed to moderate heat stress is calculated by multiplying the population living in each location by the average number of moderate heat stress days a year in this location. Moderate heat stress days are here defined as days per year where wet bulb globe temperature goes over 25°C. The size and distribution of the population are assumed to stay constant in the future, therefore this indicator only accounts for future changes in climate and not those in socioeconomic factors.
