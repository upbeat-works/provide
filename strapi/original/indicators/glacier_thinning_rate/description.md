Annual rate of glacier thinning, expressed in metre water equivalent per year (m w.e./year). A thinning rate of 1 m w.e./year over a given area means that the glaciers contained in this area have on average lost what corresponds to 1 meter of liquid water (1000 kg per square meter) over a year. The thinning rate generally increases with warming and decreases with cooling, but it can also decrease when glaciers become smaller and retreat to higher elevations.
