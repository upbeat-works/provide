Population exposed to extreme heat stress is calculated by multiplying the population living in each location by the average number of extreme heat stress days a year in this location. Extreme heat stress days are here defined as days per year where wet bulb globe temperature goes over 31°C. The size and distribution of the population are assumed to stay constant in the future, therefore this indicator only accounts for future changes in climate and not those in socioeconomic factors.
