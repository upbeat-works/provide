Population exposed to heatwaves is calculated by multiplying the population living in each location by the average number of heatwave days a year in this location. Heatwave days are here defined as the number of days among periods of at least 3 consecutive days in which the maximum and minimum temperature exceeds the 90th percentile value of a reference present period (2011-2020). The size and distribution of the population are assumed to stay constant in the future, therefore this indicator only accounts for future changes in climate and not those in socioeconomic factors.
