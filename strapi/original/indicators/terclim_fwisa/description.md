Annual maximum of the 90-day running average of the Fire Weather Index (FWI). The FWI is a meteorologically based index used worldwide to estimate fire danger. It consists of different components that account for the effects of temperature, precipitation, relative humidity and wind on fire behaviour and spread. The higher the FWI is, the more favourable the meteorological conditions to trigger a wildfire are.
