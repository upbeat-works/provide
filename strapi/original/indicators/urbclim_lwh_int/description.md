Annual number of working hours lost because of heat stress for intense activities (such as carrying heavy material, shovelling or sawing). The limits for safe working conditions under heat stress are defined by ISO 7243 and Hooyberghs et al. (Climatic Change, 2017). Intense activities are assumed to be affected as soon as wet-bulb globe temperature (WBGT) exceeds 26.6°C and to become impossible when WBGT reaches 32.6°C. WBGT is a measure of heat stress which takes into account temperature, humidity, wind speed, sun angle, shading and cloud cover.
