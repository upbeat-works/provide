Number of days per year where the Fire Weather Index (FWI) exceeds the 95th percentile of its local distribution over the pre-industrial reference period (1850-1900). The FWI is a meteorologically based index used worldwide to estimate fire danger. It consists of different components that account for the effects of temperature, precipitation, relative humidity and wind on fire behaviour and spread. The higher the FWI is, the more favourable the meteorological conditions to trigger a wildfire are.
