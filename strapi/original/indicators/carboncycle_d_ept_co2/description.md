Carbon dioxide (CO2) flux from northern peatlands. A positive value indicates CO2 emissions to the atmosphere, and a negative value indicates net CO2 uptake by the peatlands. The balance between the photosynthesis process and respiration process determines whether the peatland acts as a net carbon sink (negative CO2 flux) and a net carbon source (positive CO2 flux). Future peatland flux can be influenced by factors such as temperature change and atmospheric CO2 concentration. Changes in this indicator are expressed in petagrams of carbon per year (PgC yr-1).
