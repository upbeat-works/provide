Population exposed to high heat stress is calculated by multiplying the population living in each location by the average number of high heat stress days a year in this location. High heat stress days are here defined as days per year where wet bulb globe temperature goes over 28°C. The size and distribution of the population are assumed to stay constant in the future, therefore this indicator only accounts for future changes in climate and not those in socioeconomic factors.
