Annual number of working hours lost because of heat stress, for moderate activities (for example hammering in nails, weeding, hoeing or picking fruits or vegetables). The limits for safe working conditions under heat stress conditions are defined by ISO 7243 and Hooyberghs et al. (Climatic Change, 2017). Moderate activities are assumed to be affected as soon as wet-bulb globe temperature (WBGT) exceeds 28.6°C and to become impossible when WBGT reaches 32.8°C. WBGT is a measure of heat stress which takes into account temperature, humidity, wind speed, sun angle, shading and cloud cover.
