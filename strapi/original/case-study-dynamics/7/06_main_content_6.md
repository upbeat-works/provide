Similar trends are projected for the indicator 'lost working hours during intense activities', which quantifies the labour productivity decreases due to the challenges that heat stress will pose to physically demanding outdoor activities and jobs like those performed by construction workers. 

We show that in 2020 almost 500 working hours were already lost for intense activities in Nassau, and that this number will unavoidably exceed and at least remain slightly above that level over the course of the 21st century. Under the SSP5-8.5 scenario, decreases in labour productivity of approximately 1500-2400 hours are projected on average for each year by the end of the century (three to four times as much as in 2020).

We expect the impacts that Nassau currently faces because of heat stress, such as the difficulty to respond to the electricity demand for air conditioning, to be amplified in any future scenario, but the extent to which this is the case will strongly depend on the future levels of global emissions.
