This scenario assumes that decarbonisation is delayed to the 2030s, but then takes place in earnest. Fossil fuel use never ends but is instead compensated for with high amounts of carbon dioxide removal. After net zero greenhouse gas emissions are reached, both emissions and aerosols are kept constant to study how impacts unfold as a result. Global warming peaks at 1.7°C in 2080 then goes back to 1.4°C in 2300 (best estimate).
