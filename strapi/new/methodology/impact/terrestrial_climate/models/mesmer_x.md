MESMER-X is a statistical model based on MESMER used to emulate key extreme climate variables over land areas for given GMT trajectories. Similarly as MESMER and MESMER-M, MESMER-X can be run in different configurations that each reproduce the response of a different ESM. Currently MESMER-X is able to represent local annual maximum temperatures (TXx, [Quilcaille et al, 2022](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1029/2022GL099012)), annual average soil moisture, annual minimum of the monthly average soil moisture, and various indices related to the fire weather (see [Quilcaille et al. 2023](https://doi.org/10.5194/essd-15-2153-2023) and [Quilcaille et al. 2023](https://esd.copernicus.org/articles/14/1333/2023/) for more information). MESMER-X relies on Generalised Extreme Value theory to represent the local response of these variables to rising GMT, and is also able to explore how they are further affected by natural climate variability. MESMER-X is calibrated on existing scenario projections from ESMs, to then reproduce the behaviour of key extreme climate variables under scenarios not generated by ESMs yet. Similar to MESMER and MESMER-M, it is calibrated on projection data from each ESM individually. The current MESMER-X analyses are based on [Quilcaille et al. (2022)](https://doi.org/10.1029/2022GL099012) and [Quilcaille et al. (2023)](https://egusphere.copernicus.org/preprints/2023/egusphere-2023-589/).
