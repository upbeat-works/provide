MAGICC translates GHG emissions from the PROVIDE scenarios into a global surface air temperature (GSAT) response and into a global thermal expansion response via the hemispheric upwelling-diffusion ocean model of MAGICC. This approach reflects carbon-cycle uncertainties and the AR6 climate sensitivity ranges, by using 600 historically constrained parameter sets that have been derived using a probabilistic Metropolis–Hastings Markov chain Monte Carlo method. MAGICC-SLR calculates the GMSLR response of the global glacier mass changes, surface mass balance and solid ice discharge from the Greenland and Antarctic ice sheets on the basis of the GSAT response for each scenario.  For the population-driven land water storage component, we assume medium population growth, under the “middle-of-the-road” SSP2 land water storage setup. Calibrated sea-level parameters are randomly selected for the individual SLR component parametrizations and combined with the 600 probabilistic ensemble members.
