Number of days, hours and nights per year where wet bulb globe temperature goes over certain temperatures.

Wet bulb globe temperature (WBGT) is a measure of heat stress in direct sunlight, which takes into account temperature, humidity, wind speed, sun angle and cloud cover (solar radiation). The boundaries that we use below are standards of ISO 7243:2017 'Ergonomics of the thermal environment'. More information on these boundaries can be found in [Souverijns et al. 2023](https://rmets.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/joc.8268).

WBGT > 25°C: Moderate heat stress<br />
WBGT > 28°C: High heat stress<br />
WBGT > 29.5°C: Very high heat stress<br />
WBGT > 31°C: Extreme heat stress<br />
